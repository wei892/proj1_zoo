Sheeps

Sheep (Ovis aries) are a domesticated mammal usually kept for their livestock. An adult femail sheep is called an ewe, an adult male is called a ram, and a young sheep is called a lamb.
Sheeps, one of the most animals to be domesticated, are raised for many purposes: fleece, meat and milk. The earliest domestication is believed to fall between 11,000 and 9,000 B.C in Meospotamia.
Domestic sheeps differe from their wild relatives and ancestors in serval aspects. Domestic sheep have crimped wool and lateral spiraled horns. Another trait specific to domestic sheeps are the huge variation in colors. While wild sheeps are in various hues of brown, domestic sheeps are white to brown and even spotted. White woal is a dominant trait that spreads quickly and its popular for it commercialbility for colored fleeces. There is also variability in wool type and quality.
